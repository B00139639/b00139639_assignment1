History of the Elite series

The Elite Series began in 1984 with Elite on the BBC Micro computer with revolutionary 3D graphics, which led it to be ported to all home computer systems.

The sequel Frontier Elite 2 was release in 1993 and was the first game to feature procedurally generated star systems.

The 3rd installment, Frontier First Encounters was released in 1995. This was the first game to use procedural texturing to generate planet surfaces.

Elite Dangerous was released in 2014, after a sucessful crowd funding source in 2012. It was the first game with a persistent universe.

Elite Dangerous

Elite Dangerous is a space simulation game based in a 1 to 1 scale of the Milky Way Galaxy. Choose from 36 playable ships, to explore, fight or trade. You choose how!

Exploration

Head into the Black! With 160,000 real plotted stars and 400 billion procedurally generated stars, the galaxy is yours to discover. Find water-worlds, earth-likes, blackholes and much more on your journey.

Combat

With true 3D space combat simulation, show your skills and dominate space. With 25 different weapons in 4 class sizes, build the best ship you can with Elite.

Trading

If numbers are your thing then take a step back. Fill your hold with a commodity and sell it to another station to make as much profit as possible. Ply the trade routes with a number of trade spec ships.
